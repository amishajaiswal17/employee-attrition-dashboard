## Employee Attrition Analytics Dashboard | Excel ##
An interactive excel based HR analytics dashboard that uncovers the root causes of employee attrition across departments, roles, demographics, and behavioral patterns — enabling data-driven workforce retention strategies.

📌 Short Description

This project analyzes employee attrition data of 1,470 employees across an organization to identify who is leaving, why they might be leaving, and what the business can do about it. Built entirely in Microsoft Excel with Pivot Tables, KPI cards, and interactive slicers, it transforms raw HR data into a decision-ready dashboard for HR teams and business leaders.

🚨 Problem Statement

Employee attrition is one of the most costly challenges facing modern organizations. Studies estimate that replacing an employee costs 50–200% of their annual salary, yet most companies lack visibility into why people are leaving until it's too late.

This dashboard addresses three critical HR questions:

Who is most at risk of leaving — by age, role, department, and education?
What organizational factors drive attrition — overtime, income, job satisfaction?
Where should HR teams focus retention investments for maximum impact?


✨ Key Features & Highlights

KPI Summary Cards — Instantly surface total employees, total attrition count, attrition rate, and average age at a glance
Gender Breakdown — Visual split of male vs. female workforce distribution
Department-wise Attrition — Donut chart showing R&D, Sales, and HR attrition contribution
Attrition by Job Role — Ranked view from highest-risk (Laboratory Technician, Sales Executive) to lowest (Research Director)
Age Group Analysis — Identifies the 25–34 band as the highest attrition segment (47% of all exits)
Education Field Filter — Reveals Life Sciences and Medical graduates as top attrition groups
Marital Status Segmentation — Single employees show disproportionately high attrition (120 of 237)  
Slicers — Filter the entire dashboard dynamically by department, education field

🛠️ Tools

Microsoft Excel, Pivot Tables, Pivot Charts, Excel Slicers, Conditional Formatting, Excel Formulas

💡 Key Insights

1. 🔴 Young Professionals Are Leaving the Most
2. 🔬 Laboratory Technicians & Sales Executives Are Highest-Risk Roles
3. ⏰ Overtime Correlates with Higher Exits
4. 💍 Single Employees Leave at Nearly 2× the Rate
5. 📚 Life Sciences & Medical Backgrounds Drive Attrition
6. 🏢 R&D Bears the Highest Departmental Attrition

✅ Recommendations

Introduce structured career progression paths with transparent timelines(Age 25–34, Lab Technicians, Sales Execs)
Implement an overtime monitoring policy
Develop engagement programs tailored to single employees (flexibility, remote options)
